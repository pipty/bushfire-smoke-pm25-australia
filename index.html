Historical predictor datasets and staged modelling strategy

July 2026 progress update

Historical predictor datasets are now assembled and the staged modelling strategy has been defined. The current modelling target is monthly bushfire-smoke PM2.5 reconstruction from 1960 onward, calibrated against the modern CARE reference period.

This update extends the v0.2.0 evidence site and does not claim completed historical reconstruction or validation.

Latest Evidence Package

Modern reference period, 2000-2023

CARE modern reference-period analysis

This package characterises the modern Australian CARE daily bushfire-smoke PM2.5 product at 0.1-degree resolution. It provides the benchmark for metric derivation, event diagnostics, model testing, and later historical extension.

This is a modern reference-period analysis, not a completed historical reconstruction model.

Project Summary

Research-ready exposure evidence for long-term smoke and health studies

Dr Tingting Ye is an environmental epidemiologist based at Monash University. Through this fellowship project, she will develop a national framework to reconstruct historical bushfire-smoke PM2.5 exposure across Australia. The project combines exposure modelling, remote sensing, geospatial data integration, and environmental epidemiology to strengthen evidence on long-term smoke exposure and health risk.
